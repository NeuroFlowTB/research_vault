## What I learned
-MRI is signal measurement, not photography.
-A voxel is a small 3D signal container, not a perfect tissue label.
-Diffusion MRI measures directional water movement.
-White matter constrains water diffusion, creating anisotropy.
-Higher b-values increase diffusion sensitivity but reduce signal quality.
-dMRI preprocessing corrects noise, motion, eddy distortions, susceptibility warping, and misalignment.
-Modern neuroimaging workflows are compositional: FSL, MRtrix3, TORTOISE, and QSIPrep can interact.
